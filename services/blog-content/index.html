What's different

Why this isn't the AI-slop stuff.

Most cheap content services run a prompt through ChatGPT, run it through a paraphraser, slap a title on it, and call it a day. Google can spot that pattern now. So can your buyers. That's not what you'll get here.

01

Keywords your buyers actually search

Every post starts with real keyword research. Search volume, competition, buyer intent. The target keyword is one your customer has typed into Google in the last 30 days. Not the one a tool ranked highest for trendiness.

02

Built for the question behind the search

A post titled "Best ceramic mugs for coffee" gets written for someone deciding which mug to buy, not for a search engine. That means real product comparisons, honest opinions, and natural mentions of your products where they fit. The post reads like it was written for the buyer because it was.

03

Internal links that compound

Every post links to 2 to 4 of your existing products, collections, or related blog posts. This isn't just SEO theatre. Internal linking is how Google understands the structure of your site, and how readers find more of your stuff. Link strategy is decided post-by-post, not auto-generated.

04

Schema, indexing, the boring infrastructure

Article and BlogPosting schema applied. Meta title and description optimized for click-through. Featured image set with proper alt text. Submitted to Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools. Tagged correctly in Shopify so it shows up in your blog navigation. The boring stuff most "writers" skip.

About daily blog posts: Google doesn't care.

A common request is "can you do daily posts?" The honest answer: I can, but you probably shouldn't pay me to. Daily posting is rarely better than 2 to 3 high-quality posts per week. Google rewards depth and authority, not frequency. Burned-out content at high volume can actually hurt your rankings.

The Growth tier ($749) exists for stores running short content sprints around launches or seasonal pushes. For sustained long-term content, the Scale tier ($1,299) at 30 posts per month delivers better results at the same price.

If you specifically want daily posts, I'll do them. I'd just rather you know the trade-off first.

FAQ

Common questions about content work.

Will the posts sound like AI wrote them?

No. Every post is custom-researched and written for your specific brand and audience. AI tools are part of my workflow (research, outlining, drafting), but the final post goes through human editing for voice, accuracy, and the small considered choices that make a post readable. If you can't tell whether something was AI-written, that's because it wasn't (in the way that matters).

Do you write in my brand voice?

Yes. The first month includes a one-time brand voice document we build together. After that, every post sounds consistent. If you have an existing style guide, send it. If you don't, I build one from your product descriptions, About page, and any social media you write yourself.

How long until blog content drives traffic?

Honestly: 3 to 6 months for individual posts to rank, 6 to 12 months for compounding traffic. Content marketing is a slow channel. Anyone promising faster is either running paid ads or working in a vertical with no competition. The trade-off is that organic traffic from content keeps coming for years after the post is published, while ads stop the moment you stop paying.

Do I keep the posts if I cancel?

Yes. Once a post is published to your Shopify blog, it's yours. I don't host anything. Cancel anytime, every post stays exactly where it is.

What topics will you write about?

We agree on the editorial calendar before each month starts. I research what your buyers are searching for, what your competitors rank for, what gaps exist in your existing content, and what aligns with your products. You get a topic list to approve before any writing happens. No surprises.

Can I request specific topics?

Yes, always. Half my best topic ideas come from clients ("our customers keep asking us X" is a goldmine). Send me what you want covered. I'll build it into the calendar.
